Agriculture
in Australia

What's happening on half of Australian land area?

Exploring the 2 main parts of Australian agriculture: Livestock and Crop Production

--The Overview--

Where's the Agricultural Land?

The numbers are huge, but to put it into perspective, we need to see the ratio of agricultural land to non-agricultural land.

The stacked bar chart shows that agricultural land consistently takes up more than 50% of all Australian land.

It is often assumed that higher rainfall would correspond to greater agricultural land use.
However, the bar chart shows this is not a strong relationship.
Agricultural land distribution is influenced by more than just rainfall, including soil type, land management, and economic factors.

Here, we can see the distribution of what agricultural land is being used for.

Over 90% of agricultural land is for grazing, with only 8.6% being used for cropping.

--Crop Products--

How Crop Production Changed and Stabilised Over Time, Key Crops

The stacked area chart above shows a gradual
shift toward reduced cultivation intensity, with “no cultivation” becoming more dominant.

This reflects changing land management practices that prioritise soil conservation and long-term productivity.

This can be seen in the blue section of the chart. "No cultivation" took up only 20.9% in 1995, but rose to 73.6% as of 2011.

From this donut chart, wheat is the dominant winter crop, accounting for56.9% of winter crop production sold in 2024–25.

Below, we examine the distribution of crop production across Australian states, focusing on wheat and canola.

The very first map showed that Queensland has the largest agricultural land area, but its crop production is really low. Why is this the case?

--Livestock Product--

The Cows

The line chart shows that cattle and calves have consistently been the dominant livestock product over time,
with values more than double those of any other product.

This bar chart addresses the question raised in the previous section, showing that Queensland has by far the highest cattle numbers of any state.

This suggests that a large proportion of Queensland's agricultural land is used forgrazing.

--Food Products--

Wheat and Beef

CI stands for Confidence Interval, which gives a 95% estimated range for the true value.
The chart uses the mean of this range for each food type. Cereal-based mixed dishes stand at the top for both age groups. What does this food group represent?

The cereal-based food group includes many foods made from wheat, such as pasta, burgers, pizza, and tacos, which often contain beef as part of their common versions.
These foods are widely consumed because they combine staple crops with commonly used livestock products.

The most produced crops and livestock therefore play a major role in the everyday diet of Australians.
